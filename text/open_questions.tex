\label{sec:open}

This framing aligns with several CFP themes---\emph{safety guardrails} for
generalist policies, \emph{perceptions of safety in HRI}, \emph{safe physical
HRI} \citep{hu2022sharp}, \emph{robot jailbreaking}, and
\emph{benchmarks/evaluation}---while arguing that initiation authorization is a
missing layer in generalist-robot safety taxonomies, not a social-robot niche
concern.
Below we pose open questions and three provisional statements for community
adoption, revision, or rejection.

\paragraph{Taxonomy and measurement.}
Should ``premature commitment'' appear explicitly under social-interaction
safety in reference architectures---rather than as an afterthought of engagement
metrics?
What minimum metric set (awkwardness, interruption events, addresser accuracy,
logged margins) could compare platforms across venues?
Do we need a shared initiation-safety benchmark, or are venue-specific studies
sufficient?

\paragraph{Preference and governance.}
Who should set social risk posture---operator, user, online inference, or
regulation?
Can specialist norms unify under generalist robots, or do we need domain-specific
safety cases?
How should standards handle dense, multicultural, or multi-party scenes without
freezing robots into permanent passive-wait?

\paragraph{Foundation models and layering.}
If dialogue FMs improve fluency but not timing, where is the hard boundary
between generative planning and runtime shields \citep{ravichandran2025roboguard}?
What failures (hallucinated addressee, jailbroken greeting, over-talkative
initiation) appear only when initiation and generation are merged?
What red-team protocols apply to embodied cold-start that text-only evaluation
misses \citep{robey2024jailbreaking}?
Which initiation guarantees require runtime practice regardless of FM scale?

\paragraph{Statement 1: Initiation is safety-critical.}
For social generalist robots, \emph{when} to open a social channel is not a UX
polish layer.
It is a safety decision about irreversible commitment---analogous in structure,
if not in physics, to the workshop's context-dependent knife or egg examples.

\paragraph{Statement 2: Irreversible acts need explicit authorization.}
Starting speech, naming a user, or beginning biometric capture should not flow
unchecked from a single engagement score or an end-to-end VLA.
Authorization logic---runtime gates, probes, auditable margins---belongs in
reference architectures alongside motion-planning shields.

\paragraph{Statement 3: Auditable initiation logs should be a deployment prerequisite.}
Generalist-robot safety standards should require logged initiation decisions---
probe history, authorization margins, and first-commit timestamps---analogous
to incident logs in aviation.
Without auditable cold-start traces, post-hoc ``felt unsafe'' reports cannot
improve authorization policy after a harmful first word or first grasp.
We invite counterexamples from manipulation-centric generalists and alternative
metrics for first-commit harm.
