\subsection{Where Initiation Matters Most}

In retail, a premature greeting can interrupt a call or transactional
flow \citep{edirisinghe2024shopworker}. In homes, a generalist may
speak across an ongoing conversation. In shared labs or museums, the
same orienting move that signals availability in a demo can read as
intrusive off-hours. These scenes differ in context but share one
structure: the robot commits before it knows who is willing to be
addressed \citep{shi2011spatial,vasquez2014}.

\subsection{Open Questions}

\paragraph{Measurement and standards.} Should premature commitment
appear explicitly under social-interaction safety in reference
architectures, or stay an afterthought of engagement metrics? What
minimum metric set (awkwardness, interruption events, addressee
accuracy, logged margins) lets us compare platforms across venues?
Do we need a shared initiation-safety benchmark with scripted
latent-receptivity conditions, or are venue-specific studies
enough? Without cold-start metrics, a platform can look safe on
average while still producing frequent harmful first words in the
scenes where initiation actually occurs \citep{weiss2023perceived}.

\paragraph{Preference and governance.} Who sets social risk
posture: operator, user, online inference, or regulator? Can
specialist norms unify under generalist robots, or do we need
domain-specific safety cases? How should standards handle dense,
multicultural, or multi-party scenes without forcing robots into
permanent passive-wait? A home user may want proactive help, while
a clinic may require conservative initiation. Generalist stacks need
a dial that is legible to operators and auditable after incidents,
not a single threshold baked into model weights
\citep{liu2025embodied}.

\paragraph{Foundation models and layering.} If dialogue FMs improve
fluency but not timing, where is the hard boundary between
generative planning and runtime shields
\citep{ravichandran2025roboguard}? Which failures (hallucinated
addressee, jailbroken greeting, over-talkative initiation) appear
only when initiation and generation are merged? What red-team
protocols apply to embodied cold-start that text-only evaluation
misses \citep{robey2024jailbreaking}? Which initiation guarantees
require runtime practice regardless of FM scale? End-to-end VLAs
\citep{rt2-2023,openvla-2024,black2024pi0} make this boundary
urgent because a single rollout can couple perception, commitment,
and action.

\subsection{Statements}

\paragraph{Statement 1: Initiation is safety-critical.} For social
generalists, deciding when to open a channel is a safety decision
about irreversible commitment, analogous in structure, if not in
physics, to context-dependent knife or egg examples.

\paragraph{Statement 2: Irreversible acts need explicit
authorization.} Starting speech, naming a user, or beginning
biometric capture should not flow unchecked from a single
engagement score or an end-to-end VLA. Authorization
logic---runtime gates, probes, auditable margins---belongs in
reference architectures alongside motion shields.

\paragraph{Statement 3: Initiation logs should be a deployment
prerequisite.} Generalist-robot safety standards should require
logged initiation decisions (probe history, authorization margins,
first-commit timestamps), analogous to incident logs in aviation.
Without auditable cold-start traces, ``felt unsafe'' reports cannot
improve policy after a harmful first word or first grasp.
