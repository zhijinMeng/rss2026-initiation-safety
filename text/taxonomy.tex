\subsection{Taxonomy Placement}

Physical safety (collision, force, joint limits) remains necessary
\citep{akalin2021,zhang2025safevla,kojima2025fmr}.
Cyber and privacy safety (data leakage, jailbreak prompting) are increasingly
salient for foundation-model robots \citep{robey2024jailbreaking,bai2022constitutional}.
For robots that initiate contact in public or domestic space, we argue that
\textbf{social-interaction safety} must be explicit---and that
\textbf{initiation authorization} is a critical subclass within it.

\noindent
HRI research distinguishes \emph{physical} from \emph{perceived} safety
\citep{akalin2021,weiss2023perceived}; social-error taxonomies catalog
socio-affective failures \citep{tian2021socialerrors}, yet rarely specify an
explicit \textbf{authorization layer} before the first commitment
\citep{shi2011spatial,edirisinghe2024shopworker}.
Initiation authorization is a \emph{decision-layer} concern---a
\textbf{pre-interaction safety layer} added to that lineage.

\paragraph{Beyond speech.}
On speech-capable platforms the commitment may be the first word; on
manipulation-centric VLAs it may be the first grasp of a contested object, an
uninvited approach into personal space, or a clarifying utterance that interrupts
a human--human exchange \citep{rt2-2023,openvla-2024,huang2016anticipatory}.
The common structure is \emph{irreversibility under uncertainty}, not modality.

\subsection{Workshop Themes and Generalist Assumptions}

The workshop overview stresses that safety depends on context, intent, and human
expectation rather than constraint enforcement alone.
Table~\ref{tab:workshop-map} translates those examples to initiation.

Specialist deployments embed assumptions that generalist robots cannot rely on:
\textbf{(i)}~one threshold $\theta$ for all users;
\textbf{(ii)}~engagement score as a proxy for intent without probe responses
\citep{salam2017};
\textbf{(iii)}~symmetric treatment of false act and false wait; and
\textbf{(iv)}~static scripts across venues.
Generalists need \emph{parametric} authorization: venue- and user-specific risk
posture without retraining the entire VLA stack \citep{liu2025embodied}.

\subsection{Layered Safety and Literature Boundaries}

Unification may require \emph{layered} safety: physical shields and
shielding-aware motion planning \citep{hu2022sharp}; VLA alignment
\citep{zhang2025safevla,kojima2025fmr}; and \textbf{initiation shields}
\citep{ravichandran2025roboguard} added \emph{before} the first irreversible
social commitment.
Recent FMR guardrails reduce unsafe \emph{plans} and jailbroken commands, but
typically assume the robot is already authorized to attempt the commanded
action---they do not resolve whether the robot should open the social channel in
the first place \citep{kojima2025fmr,ravichandran2025roboguard,robey2024jailbreaking}.

\paragraph{Why this is a safety paper, not engagement.}
Reviewers may ask whether we merely relabel proactive HRI
\citep{broek2024proactive,hoffman2007anticipatory,shi2011spatial,bergstrom2008encounters}.
Proactive-robot research studies \emph{how} to initiate efficiently---timing,
position, and naturalness; we study \emph{whether} initiative is permitted under
uncertainty.

\paragraph{vs.\ physical safety and VLA guardrails.}
Collision avoidance and SafeVLA-style constraints answer: \emph{Will the motion
harm someone?} \citep{zhang2025safevla}
Initiation authorization answers: \emph{Should the robot commit an irreversible
social act now?}
A robot can be collision-free yet socially harmful---the workshop's knife
near-miss is the physical analogue \citep{akalin2021}.

\paragraph{vs.\ preference alignment.}
Preference alignment optimizes \emph{what} a model says or does \emph{after} a
channel is open \citep{bai2022constitutional}.
Embodied cold-start differs: low-cost probes change human state, harm is social
and bidirectional, and irreversible commitment precedes correction.
Red-teaming a dialogue model does not substitute for probe--response dynamics on
a physical robot \citep{robey2024jailbreaking}.

\paragraph{vs.\ engagement engineering.}
Engagement scores optimize interaction quality \citep{rich2010,salam2017}; they
do not constitute consent.
Safety research must allow \textbf{refraining} from interaction when evidence is
insufficient; an engagement-optimal policy may speak or act early, an
initiation-safe policy may not.
