We instantiate this initiation shield as \textsc{pas}
(Probe--Authorize--Speak) on SocialVLA-Engage.

\begin{algorithm}[h]
\caption{Initiation authorization loop}
\small
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State \textbf{Input:} cues $e_t$, probe history, venue risk posture
\State \textbf{Invariant:} probe module $\not\to$ \textsc{speak}
\If{authorization gate closed}
  \State emit reversible probe or wait, then update receptivity evidence
\ElsIf{gate open}
  \State permit FM dialogue / first \textsc{speak} and log decision margin
\EndIf
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{Runtime data flow.} Each cycle, perception updates
engagement cues \citep{rich2010,salam2017}.
The probe module emits only reversible nonverbal actions
(\texttt{wait}, \texttt{head}, \texttt{orient}) and is prevented
from emitting \texttt{speak}.
Evidence accumulates from how the person responds
\citep{skantze2014speech,bohus2014}.
The authorization gate is the sole path to \texttt{speak}, so even
high $e_t$ does not produce speech if the gate is closed.
Only after authorization does a foundation-model dialogue stack run.
Passive direct-init ($e_t > \theta$) bypasses this split and can
speak when refraining is socially safer. Any architecture
enforcing probe~$\to$~authorize~$\to$~act would instantiate the
same principles.

\paragraph{Study scenario.} Participants encounter a humanoid at a
scripted doorway. The visitor may be on a phone, mid-conversation,
or clearly receptive \citep{edirisinghe2024shopworker}. Each session
logs probe history, gate state, and the timestamp of first
\textsc{speak}, supporting mechanistic and subjective comparison
across arms.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol}

We evaluate three initiation policies in a between-subjects study
($n{=}90$, ethics-approved). All three arms share hardware, the same
perception stack, and the same post-initiation dialogue stack. Only
the initiation policy differs:

\paragraph{Endpoints.} \emph{Mechanistic:} distribution of the
speak-margin proxy at first speak ($\Delta b_{\text{init}}$),
comparing \textsc{pas} with direct-init. \emph{User-reported:}
conversation success, awkwardness, and naturalness on 7-point
Likert scales. \emph{Deployment:} risk-posture sweep on
\textsc{pas} without retraining perception.

\paragraph{Falsifiers.} The framing is testable: if \textsc{pas} and
direct-init produce indistinguishable margin distributions, the
architectural split adds little.
If awkwardness does not track margin alignment, felt safety likely
needs metrics beyond timing proxies
\citep{tian2021socialerrors}.
If risk-posture settings do not monotonically trade initiation rate
against margin, deployment-time authorization is not a viable safety
dial.
The study measures whether initiation authorization is manipulable
and whether margin distributions differ across policy classes.
