Generalist-robot safety must include \emph{initiation
authorization}: deciding whether a robot may commit its first
irreversible social act under uncertainty. An engagement score
measures apparent receptivity. It does not answer whether the robot
should commit that first act when the signal is ambiguous
\citep{rich2010,salam2017,broek2024proactive,shi2011spatial}.

Deployed stacks often collapse three distinct safety decisions into
one pipeline. \emph{Physical safety} constrains motion---collision,
force, joint limits \citep{zhang2025safevla,kojima2025fmr}.
\emph{Social-interaction safety} governs how the robot behaves once
interaction has started
\citep{akalin2021,weiss2023perceived,tian2021socialerrors}.
\emph{Initiation authorization} governs whether to start at all and
is largely absent from reference architectures. The harm often appears
as an ordinary first move: a mistimed greeting that interrupts a call
\citep{edirisinghe2024shopworker}, or an end-to-end VLA that reaches
for a contested object before assent
\citep{rt2-2023,openvla-2024,black2024pi0}.

Most deployed stacks treat initiation as a fixed threshold:
$\textsc{act/speak} \Leftrightarrow e_t > \theta$
\citep{bohus2014}. That treats an engagement score, or a single VLA
rollout, as social permission, with no context, no preference, and
no evidence from low-cost probes
\citep{skantze2014speech,vasquez2014,bohus2014}.

initiation safety as \emph{safety-by-practice}: reversible probes
before any irreversible commitment, explicit risk posture, and
runtime shields before foundation-model dialogue.

Building on this distinction, we make three contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
\item We name \emph{initiation authorization} as a distinct safety
layer, separate from physical shields, post-plan VLA guardrails, and
engagement optimization.
\item We sketch \textsc{pas} (probe--authorize--speak) on a physical
robot and propose an evaluation protocol that compares three
initiation policies with explicit falsifiers.
\item We pose open questions on metrics, deployment logging,
governance, and the boundary between initiation and foundation-model
generation.
\end{enumerate}
