Generalist robots need safety not only in motion and dialogue, but in deciding
whether an interaction's first \emph{irreversible commitment}---a spoken
greeting, uninvited grasp, or approach---should occur at all.
We call this missing layer \textbf{initiation authorization}, a subclass of
social-interaction safety under-specified in current taxonomies
\citep{akalin2021,weiss2023perceived,tian2021socialerrors}.
Most stacks treat engagement score or a VLA rollout as permission
($e_t > \theta \Rightarrow \texttt{ACT/SPEAK}$), though observation is not
consent.
We position initiation authorization within generalist-robot safety, contrast
post-plan VLA guardrails \citep{zhang2025safevla,kojima2025fmr,ravichandran2025roboguard,robey2024jailbreaking}
with a missing pre-commitment layer, sketch PAS (probe--authorize--speak) on a
real robot, and pose workshop questions; study outcomes are \textbf{in progress}.
