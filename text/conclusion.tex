\label{sec:conclusion}

Generalist-robot safety is broader than constraint enforcement.
Our core claim is that safety must include \textbf{initiation authorization}:
deciding whether a robot is permitted to commit its first irreversible social act
under uncertainty---by speech, reach, or approach.
On many platforms the harmful act is that first commitment, not a collision;
mapping workshop themes to pre-commitment decisions clarifies why passive
thresholds and end-to-end VLAs are structurally misaligned with generalist
deployment \citep{rt2-2023,zhang2025safevla,ravichandran2025roboguard}.

A brief PAS sketch shows the framing is implementable; ongoing studies ask
whether it is measurable and felt by users.
The larger goal is community discussion: metrics, benchmarks, FM boundaries, and
governance of social risk posture.
We hope initiation authorization becomes a peer topic to physical safety as
foundation-model stacks move into public and domestic space.
